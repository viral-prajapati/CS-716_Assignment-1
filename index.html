About the Program

MSc Computer Science - Data Science

The Master of Science in Data Science at the University of Regina is designed to equip students with the skills and knowledge to tackle complex data science challenges. The program focuses on key areas of data science including statistical analysis, machine learning, data mining, and data visualization.

This interdisciplinary program is offered through the Department of Computer Science and provides students with the flexibility to explore a variety of data science applications across different fields.

Curriculum

The MSc in Data Science program at the University of Regina provides a well-rounded curriculum that covers key concepts and advanced techniques in data science. The program is structured to provide both theoretical knowledge and practical skills, preparing students for a wide range of data science careers. A fully-qualified student has to take 30 credits of courses to earn a Master's in Data Science. Below are the core and elective courses that make up the program.

MSc in Computer Science - Data Science (30 credit hours)

1. First Semester (Winter):

  • STAT 850: Statistical Inference (3 cr hrs)

    This course explores statistical models and their applications to data science. Students will learn about hypothesis testing, regression analysis, and Bayesian inference methods.

  • CS 841: Data Mining and Knowledge Discovery (3 cr hrs)

    Students will study the principles of data mining, including algorithms for clustering, classification, and pattern discovery. The course also covers ethical issues in data mining.

    • 2. Second Semester (Spring/Summer):

  • CS 870: Data Visualization (3 cr hrs)

    This course teaches the fundamentals of visualizing large and complex datasets, emphasizing the design of intuitive and interactive visual representations. Tools such as D3.js and Tableau are covered.

  • CS 820: Database Systems and Management (3 cr hrs)

    This course focuses on the design, development, and management of database systems. Topics include SQL, NoSQL, database security, and optimization techniques for large-scale databases.

    • 3. Third Semester (Fall):

  • CS 831: Advanced Machine Learning (3 cr hrs)

    This course focuses on modern machine learning techniques, including deep learning, neural networks, and reinforcement learning. Students will gain hands-on experience with popular machine learning frameworks like TensorFlow and PyTorch.

  • CS 832: Big Data Analytics (3 cr hrs)

    This course covers the tools and techniques used to process and analyze large datasets. Topics include Hadoop, Spark, distributed computing, and cloud-based data processing platforms.

    • 4. Fourth Semester (Fall):

  • CS 860: Cloud Computing for Data Science (3 cr hrs)

    In this course, students learn how to leverage cloud computing platforms such as AWS and Google Cloud for scalable data science applications. The course covers cloud architecture, storage, and deployment of data science pipelines.

  • CS 851: Artificial Intelligence (3 cr hrs)

    This course explores key concepts in artificial intelligence, including reasoning, planning, natural language processing, and robotics. Students will work on AI models and applications in various domains.

    • 5. Fifth Semester (Winter):

  • CS 818: Data Science Thesis (6 cr hrs)

    Under the guidance of a faculty member, students can carry out original research in a field of interest through their thesis.

    • or

  • CS 819: Data Science Project (6 cr hrs)

    The Data Science Stream's capstone project will be a milestone-based project that will be pursued. The completed projects will be shown and given a public presentation.

    • Thesis/Project

    As part of the MSc in Data Science program, students must complete either a thesis or a major project. The thesis allows students to conduct original research in an area of their interest under the supervision of a faculty member. Alternatively, students may choose the project option, which involves applying data science techniques to solve a real-world problem. The project is typically carried out in collaboration with an industry partner, providing valuable experience in the application of data science in a professional setting.

    Admissions

    The MSc Data Science program is open to applicants with a strong background in computer science, mathematics, or a related field. Applicants must meet the following requirements:

    • A bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Mathematics, or a related discipline.
    • A minimum GPA of 75% in the last two years of undergraduate study.
    • Proficiency in programming languages such as Python, R, or Java.
    • Two academic references.

    Application Deadlines

    • Winter intake: July 1

    Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

    How long does the program take to complete?

    The MSc Data Science program can be completed in 2 years full-time or 3-4 years part-time.

    Can I pursue the program part-time?

    Yes, the program offers flexibility for part-time students.

    What career opportunities are available after graduation?

    Graduates of the MSc Data Science program can pursue roles such as Data Scientist, Machine Learning Engineer, Data Analyst, and Big Data Specialist in various industries.
